Venba and Paavalan are Indian immigrants struggling to find the “better life” they’d hoped for in Canada. The game tells their story through short animations, and you, the player, sometimes get to choose the direction of dialogue. The exciting part, though, is when you get to help Venba cook.

Venba recreates her mother’s dishes, satisfying cravings for home and attempting to pass Tamil culture onto her son Kavin. But her cookbook is damaged, so you have to help her decode memories, or just guess whether cardamom pods or ground turmeric goes in the biryani gravy first.

## About the creator
Visai Games is a small, Toronto-based studio (Visai Studios, n.d.). Abhi, _Venba’s_ designer, programmer, and writer, moved from Tamil Nadu to Toronto with his parents at age 12 (Ahmed, 2021). Sam Elkana is the game’s art director, and Shahrin Khan its producer and writer. 

## Critical evaluation
_Venba_ is the name of the game. Venba is the name of our main character. And venba is a form of classical Tamil poetry (Visai Games, 2023), which is fitting, because the entire game feels poetic. It’s packaged into seven chapters, a rhythm of short narratives and cooking puzzles. A tight script delivers deep emotions: fear when your dependable husband doesn’t come home from work, sadness when your child says he doesn’t want to bring your home-cooked food to college.

Though the game can be played in less than two hours, it’s rich with detail. Each chapter opens with a venba by the prolific Tamil poet Thiruvalluvar. Rather than telling you they’re worried about money, the game shows Venba sleeping with a free airline blanket, Paavalan wearing his glasses for years after they’re cracked. Bold, retro art is paired with satisfying sound effects — the hiss of a pressure cooker, the crackle of oil in a hot pan — as well as a triumphant [original soundtrack](https://music.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL1aefs7lqH86kVEBmrkfVFGeEy3wxr0U3), including a song by the iconic Tamil cinema composer Deva (Carpenter, 2023).

My favorite thing about _Venba_ is that it plays from the mother’s perspective. “I personally find a lot of media that relates to immigrant stories, they tend to focus on the second generation kids,” writer Abhi said. “But I think the parents have the cooler sorry to tell.”

## Library program
_Bind your own cookbook._ Invite teens to bring their family recipes to the library. Help them design recipe pages, then demonstrate a few simple bookbinding methods using staples, tape, or needle and thread. Send them home with completed cookbooks, leaving a few pages blank for future recipes.

## Speed-round book talk
Venba and Paavalan immigrate from India to Canada, hoping to give their son Kavin the best possible opportunities. Cooking becomes a way for Venba to stay connected to home, and a way to pass her heritage onto Kavin, but she needs to decode the recipes in her oil-stained cookbook — and you can help by solving colorful kitchen puzzles. _Venba_ is a celebration of Tamil culture, but you don’t need to know that to enjoy its heartwarming narrative, satisfying gameplay, and stellar soundtrack.

## Potential challenges
_Venba_ celebrates diversity and touches on immigrant experiences of racism, but its story is not particularly subversive. I don’t anticipate challenges.

## Reason for inclusion
“This game was healing, therapeutic, and made me call my mom,” one player wrote (Retroid_BiPoCket, 2024). Though _Venba_ plays from the perspective of an immigrant mother, it tells the story of Kavin’s growth, and it explores identity and family relationships in a fun format for teens.
