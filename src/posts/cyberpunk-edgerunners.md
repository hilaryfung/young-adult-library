## Interest level
Ages 17 and up

## Summary 
It’s 2075 in Night City, a dystopian California metropolis. Street violence is rampant, people are obsessed with cybernetic body modification, and a handful of megacorporations run everything from schools to hospitals. After 17-year-old David Martinez loses his mom and gets expelled from the prestigious Arasaka Academy, he survives the streets by becoming a mercenary — also known as an edgerunner.

This anime series extends the universe from video game _Cyberpunk 2077_, which itself was built on the _Cyberpunk_ tabletop game created by Mike Pondsmith in 1988 (Ostler, 2022). Studio Trigger is working on another standalone series, _Cyberpunk: Edgerunners 2._

## About the director
Hiroyuki Imaishi is a Japanese anime director known for _Gurren Lagann_ and _Promare_ (Anime News Network, n.d.) He worked at Gainax before founding Studio Trigger in 2011.

## Critical evaluation
_Cyberpunk: Edgerunners_ is fast-paced and thrilling. The dystopian universe danced with the limits of my fear, especially when its dystopian premise touched on our current reality. In a fictional 2075, corporations go to war to control healthcare; in real-world 2026, Americans face healthcare market consolidation and decreasing life expectancies (Malina et al., 2025). Night City residents pay for pleasurable “brain dances” that tax their bodies; real teens in the U.S. connect with friends through social media even though it hurts their mental health (Faverio et al., 2025).

I tolerated these technofeudalist horrors because despite them all, David and his friends try to take care of each other. The dark world of _Cyberpunk_ moved me to appreciate and fight for everything we haven’t yet lost. The electrifying original soundtrack helps, too.

## Library program
_Anime club._ I wouldn’t screen _Cyberpunk: Edgerunners_ at a library, especially not around younger high schoolers. But plenty of anime series are more appropriate and streamable on Netflix or Crunchyroll. Get a license, make some popcorn, and let teens relax with _Pokemon_, _Dragon Ball Z_, or _Frieren_.

## Reason for inclusion
Anime and dystopia are both very popular with many teens. I wanted to include this exploration of capitalism, tech, and mental health through a genre that helps teens process big emotions (Nadworny, 2017). I also included the series because it has an upcoming standalone sequel (Dempsey, 2026).
