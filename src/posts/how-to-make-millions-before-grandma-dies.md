## Interest level
Ages 13 and up.

## Plot summary 
Mengju Saejiu lives in Bangkok’s Chinatown and wakes up before dawn every morning to sell congee. When she’s diagnosed with cancer, her three children barely conceal their interest in inheriting her house. To everyone’s surprise, Mengju’s grandson M ends up spending the most time with her. The college dropout and video game streamer moves into her house, learns to make congee, and takes her to chemotherapy appointments. Of course, he’s interested in his inheritance, too.

## About the director
Pat Boonnitipat is a Thai director with Cantonese and Hakka ancestry (Yiting, 2024).

## Critical evaluation
I’ve never seen anything like _How To Make Millions Before Grandma Dies_, which reminded me of my family, not only because the characters are Thai Teochew. Cultural specificities make the film feel universal: The grandmother doesn’t want anything on her Iron Goddess altar to move; the grandson offends her by installing a security camera.

Assaratanakul embodies the tenderness and rebellion of young adulthood, while Seamkhum acts with strength and sass even as her character understands the finality of her diagnosis. The film is sentimental, but it made me laugh, too, like when M modifies his grandmother’s saying: “The early bird gets the worm, when the worm is in a different time zone.”

## Library program
_Pomegranate time capsule._ In the film, Mengju has a pomegranate tree that was planted the same year M was born. Stream the movie, then lead an activity where teens research the day or year of their birth. Can they find a local building that’s the same age as them? What was on the front page of the newspaper on their birth date? What pop hits came out that year?
