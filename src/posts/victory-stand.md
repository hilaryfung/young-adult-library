## Plot summary 
Tommie Smith is a track and field athlete known for raising his fist — a symbol of Black power — while standing on a podium at the 1968 Olympics. He tells his story in this graphic memoir, starting with his childhood on a Texas farm. In high school in California’s Central Valley, he starts setting athletic records and gets recruited to run for San José State University. There, he studies hard and excels , but he also experiences and witnesses racial discrimination. ## Library program
Write your own graphic memoir. Teens are invited to write personal stories in comic form. They may create simple two-panel comics or longer zines. Provide a variety of paper, drawing and coloring utensils, and if possible, scrap material such as magazines weeded from the library’s collection. Encourage them not to stress about their drawing skills. Stick figures can tell a story! Provide prompts related to the book, such as:

* Write about your childhood home
* Write about someone who always cheers you on
* Write about a time that you felt proud or triumphant
* Write about a person or conversation that inspired you
* Write about an experience that felt unfair
* Write about a time when you weren’t sure what you were going to do

## Reason for inclusion
The book also supports young people who are finding their role in society. Smith grows throughout the book from being unsure of himself, feeling like a “country boy” to taking the world stage. When he starts excelling at SJSU he faces a decision - whether to stand with march with people or run a track meet. He learns how to be an individual while also acting in cooperation with others.
